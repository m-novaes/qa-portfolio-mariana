# **Software Development Life Cycle Methodologies - SDLC**

## 1. Waterfall Model (or line sequential model)

- Requirement Analysis > System Design > Implementation > Testing > Deployment > Maintenance

## 2. Prototyping Methodology

- Requirement Gatherins > *Design > Prototype > Custumer Evaluation > Refined Prototype* > Final Product

## 3. Spiral Methodology

- Planning > *Risk Analysis* > Engineering > Evaluation

## 4. Rapid Application Development

-  Requirement Planning (requirement gathering and analysis) > *User Design (prototype) > Construction (development)* > Cutover (implementation)

## 5. Rational Unified Process Methodology

- Inception Phase (project scope definition) > Elaboration Phase (project requirements + architecture definition) > Construction Phase (developers code)> Transition Phase (product deliver)

- Workflows of each phase: Business Modeling (business context) > Requirement > Analysis & Design > Implementation > Testing > Deployment

## 6. Agile Software Development Methodology

- Flexibility and an adaptive approach while developing a product.
- The next feature is taken up in the next iteration and is developed on the previously developed feature. 
- After every iteration, the working product is delivered to the customer for their feedback and each iteration lasts for 2-4 weeks.
- Requiriment gathering & analysis > Design > Coding > Testing > Iteration > Custumer feedback

## 7. Scrum Development Methodology

- It is an iterative and incremental *agile software development* framework.
- It is a more time-boxed and planned method.
- It is best suited for Projects in which requirements are not clear and keep on changing rapidly.
- Scrum Master: helps to successfully deliver the Sprint goals.
- The backlog is defined as the work to be done as a priority. 
- The backlog items are completed in small sprints that last for 2-4 weeks.
- Scrum meeting is done on a daily basis to explain the progress of backlogs and to discuss possible obstacles.

## 8. Lean Development Methodology

- Used in software development to decrease cost, effort, and waste.
- Identify Value (product to be delivered at a specfic time and cost) > Map the Value (requirements of the product) > Create Flow (delivering on time of customer needs) > Establish Pull (product as per the customer’s needs) > Seek Perfection (delivering a product as expected by the customer within the time allocated and cost decided)
- Lean Development principals:
1. Waste elimination
2. Amplifying learning
3. Late decicion making
4. Fast delivery
5. Team empowerment
6. Built-in integrity
7. View application as a whole

## 9. Extreme Programming Methodology (XP Methodology)

- It is used to create software wherein the requirement is not stable.
- It focuses on reducing the cost of the software with continuous testing & planning. 
- It provides iterative and frequent releases throughout the SDLC phases of the Project.
- Core Practices:
1. Fine-scale feedback
2. Continuous Process
3. Shared understanding
4. Programmer welfare


## SDLC Methodologies (Summary)

I studied 9 major SDLC methodologies. Here is a quick comparison of the most relevant ones for QA:

| Methodology | Key Characteristic | QA Focus |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Waterfall** | Linear, sequential phases. | Testing happens late (after Dev). Hard to change. |
| **Agile** | Iterative, flexible, customer feedback every 2-4 weeks. | QA is involved from the start (Requirements). Continuous testing. |
| **Scrum** | Agile framework with Sprints (2-4 weeks), Daily Standups, Backlog. | QA participates in Sprint Planning and Reviews. |
| **XP (Extreme Programming)** | Focus on continuous testing, frequent releases, pair programming. | Testing is core to the process (TDD - Test Driven Development). |
| **Lean** | Focus on eliminating waste and delivering value fast. | QA focuses on value-added testing, avoiding unnecessary checks. |

> **Key Takeaway:** In modern environments (Agile/Scrum), the QA role shifts from "finding bugs at the end" to "preventing bugs from the start" by participating in Requirements and Design phases.
